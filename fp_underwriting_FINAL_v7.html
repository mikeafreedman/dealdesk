3 — Underwriting Assumptions
Income, Growth & Vacancy
Core underwriting parameters for revenue and expense projections
Vacancy & Bad Debt Rate
Combined vacancy and credit loss as a percentage of GPR. Applied as a deduction to arrive at Effective Gross Income.
Excel default: 7.5% (incl. bad debt)
Annual Rent Growth
Year-over-year percentage increase applied to all rental income beginning in Year 2.
Philadelphia metro avg: 2.5–3.5%
Annual Expense Growth
Year-over-year percentage increase applied to all operating expense line items beginning in Year 2.
CPI-based; Excel default: 3.0%
Loss to Lease
Percentage discount from market rent representing the gap between in-place rents and current market rents.
Excel default: 3.0%
Exit Cap Rate
Capitalization rate applied to Year N+1 NOI to calculate terminal sale value. Typically 25–50 bps above entry cap.
Excel default: 7.0%
Monthly Rent (In-Place)
Total monthly rent if property is stabilized or leased. Leave 0 if vacant — system will use market data.
0 = use extracted rent roll / market
CAM Reimbursements — Year 1
Tenant reimbursements for Common Area Maintenance. Added to Effective Gross Income.
NNN recoveries — Year 1
Fee Income — Year 1
Miscellaneous fee income (laundry, parking, pet, application, late fees, etc.).
Ancillary income — Year 1

7 — Initial Financing
Acquisition Loan Parameters
Loan sizing, terms, and origination fee for the acquisition loan
Loan-to-Value (LTV)
LTV ratio for the acquisition loan. The model uses the lower of this LTV or the DSCR-constrained maximum.
Agency range: 65–75%
Interest Rate
Annual interest rate on the acquisition loan. Used to calculate monthly debt service and DSCR.
Adjust to current market conditions
Amortization Period
Full amortization schedule in years — determines P&I payment size alongside the interest rate.
Most commercial loans: 25–30 yrs
Loan Term
Balloon / maturity term of the acquisition loan in years.
Typical: 5, 7, or 10-year term
Interest-Only Period
Months of interest-only payments before principal amortization begins.
0 = fully amortizing from day 1
Origination Fee
Lender origination fee as a percentage of the initial loan amount. Applied as a financing cost in Sources & Uses.
% of initial loan amount — dollar amount auto-calculated
Loan amount × origination fee %
Closing Costs
Lump-sum closing costs for the acquisition loan (lender legal, title, recording, etc.). Applied as a financing cost in Sources & Uses.
Enter dollar amount — added directly to Uses
