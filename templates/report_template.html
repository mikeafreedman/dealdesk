How this price was derived
The underwrite-to price is solved by binary-searching the purchase price between 25% and 200% of the {{ "$" + "{:,.0f}".format(price_solver.base_purchase_price) }} current underwritten basis. At each candidate price, a 2,000-path Monte Carlo simulation perturbs four market-sensitive inputs — rent growth (σ ≈ 1.5%), exit cap rate (σ ≈ 1.0%, clipped 2–15%), vacancy (σ ≈ 2.5%), and expense growth (σ ≈ 1.0%) — around their underwritten means, while holding debt terms, amortization, hold period, capex schedule, and the LP/GP waterfall fixed. LP IRR at each trial is derived from project IRR plus the deterministic promote offset, which is stable across the search range because the waterfall does not depend on purchase price. The search converges within up to 15 iterations and a 20 bps IRR tolerance on the price at which the median LP IRR across the 2,000 paths equals the {{ solved_price_target_irr_pct or "15%" }} target — the figure reported above.
